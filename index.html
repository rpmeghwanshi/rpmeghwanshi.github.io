About Me

I am a final-year (5th) Ph.D. student in the Electrical Engineering Department at the Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur , supervised by Dr. Amit Bhardwaj and Dr. Himanshu Kumar .

My research focuses on human kinesthetic force perception and psychophysics , investigating perceptual mechanisms and developing computational models for applications in teleoperation, human–robot interaction, and perception-aware haptic systems.

Research Interests

Kinesthetic Force Perception

Understanding human perception and discrimination of one- and multi-dimensional force stimuli.

Haptic Psychophysics

Experimental investigation and computational modeling of human haptic perception.

Multisensory Perception

Studying human perception and integration of sensory information during object interaction.

Perception-Aware Haptic Systems

Applying human perceptual models to teleoperation and human–robot interaction.

Research

I study human perception of force and multisensory integration for object interaction, combining psychophysics, computational modeling, and human-centered experimentation. My recent work explores:

  • Effect of Round-Trip Delay in TPTA ( ACM SAP'26 )
  • Multi-Dimensional Perceptual Deadzone ( ACM TAP'26 )
  • Effect of Continuous Transition on Force Discrimination ( IEEE WHC'25 )
  • Effect of Force-Rate on Kinesthetic Force Discrimination ( Experimental Brain Research'25 )
